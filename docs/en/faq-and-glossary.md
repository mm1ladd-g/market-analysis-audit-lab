# FAQ and glossary

[English](faq-and-glossary.md) · [فارسی](../fa/faq-and-glossary.md) · [Documentation](index.md)

**Is the percentage a win rate?** No. It is weighted scenario-outcome alignment among counted, activated, verifiable claims.

**Does the tool prove an analyst is good or profitable?** No. It evaluates a declared subset of claims under a declared evidence policy.

**Can I audit any public video?** Public visibility is not permission. Confirm platform terms, rights, law, model submission, storage, and publication rights first.

**Why exclude a scenario that did not trigger?** A conditional statement makes no testable directional commitment until its condition occurs.

**Why can two reruns differ?** Platforms, feeds, dependencies, models, and probabilistic responses change. Preserve accepted artifacts and verify hashes.

**Should I use yfinance?** It is convenient but unofficial and often proxy-grade. Prefer exact licensed CSV/provider data for high-stakes publication.

**Does `store=false` mean no provider retention?** Do not infer that. Read current provider documentation and account terms.

## Glossary

- **Canonical evidence:** application-copied text from the hashed transcript line range.
- **Collection ID:** identifier derived from the frozen source snapshot.
- **Counted denominator:** only correct, partial, and incorrect claims with adequate evidence.
- **Deterministic exclusion:** non-model rule removing an ineligible claim before interpretation.
- **Invalidation:** condition that makes a scenario no longer valid.
- **Manifest:** inclusion/exclusion ledger and source metadata.
- **Proxy:** a different instrument or venue used as an explicitly disclosed approximation.
- **Scoreability:** whether and under what trigger a claim can be evaluated.
- **Hash-verifiable:** later byte changes are detectable when compared with the recorded hashes; this is not an independent timestamp, signature, or proof that the original capture was complete.
- **Right of reply:** reasonable opportunity for a named subject to address factual evidence errors or add a response.
