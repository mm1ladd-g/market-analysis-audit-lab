# Code of Conduct

[English](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) · [فارسی](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.fa.md)

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity or expression, experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We will act in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Expected behavior

- Demonstrate empathy and kindness.
- Respect differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Give and accept constructive, evidence-based feedback.
- Take responsibility, apologize when necessary, and learn.
- Focus on what is best for the community and the people affected by audits.
- Protect private data, source rights, and the dignity of subjects and contributors.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Sexualized language or attention, trolling, insults, personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment, threats, stalking, or doxxing.
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission.
- Defamation, deceptive evidence presentation, harassment of an audited subject, or attempts to manipulate engagement.
- Using project channels to solicit financial trades, promise profit, or target vulnerable people.
- Conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may clarify standards and take fair corrective action, including editing or removing content, warnings, temporary restrictions, or permanent bans. Report conduct privately through the repository's maintainer contact or GitHub moderation tools. Do not include sensitive evidence in a public issue.

Maintainers will respect reporter privacy as far as reasonably possible, avoid conflicts of interest, document enforcement decisions internally, and apply standards consistently. Retaliation against a reporter is prohibited.

## Scope

This policy applies in project spaces and when an individual officially represents the project in public spaces.
